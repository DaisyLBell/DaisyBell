Each zine designer pitched one or more color palettes that they thought best represented the zine’s theme. The second issue’s theme was the identity spectrum. This is the color palette that the zine designers thought best represented Spectrum.
Moodboard
In the weeks following the color palette selection, the zine designers collaborated on one collective mood board. By creating a shared moodboard, the designers were able to form relationships with each other by aligning their unique visions. This exercise was critical to ensure visually cohesive graphics in the zine.

For the Renaissance theme, the zine designers selected a much more colorfully limited palette than for Spectrum. While the color palette above was the final color palette selected, almost all of the palettes involved earth tones and subtly nuanced feels.
Moodboard
Where I was hands-off in the collaboration of the Spectrum zine’s moodboard, I was not with this zine’s moodboard. During weekly creative meetings, I challenged the zine designers to imagine a new non-Western-centric Renaissance aesthetic. What geometric motifs could nod to Renaissance art while adapting to a 21st century feel? This question helped us find visual inspiration in K-Pop posters and skin-care advertisements.
