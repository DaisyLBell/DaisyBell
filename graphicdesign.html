Practice Makes Perfect!

As a designer, I make time to brush up on different software skills to ensure I can create captivating visuals with the most cutting-ege technology. These are a few "mock" marketing assets I created which showcase my ability to work in and out of multiple softwares.

The first "mock" assets underline my ability to work with 3D modeling software. While I have experience with the CAD softwares Solidworks and AutoCAD, and the modeling software Maya, I used Blender to create this graphic. I wanted to practice my ability to make real-life products and use lighting to make them shine.

For this project, I wanted to create a product that aligns with the current internet culture, and that is skincare. We all have skin and we all struggle to take care of it. In order to create a sophisticated feel to the model and align it with current packaging design trends, I made the exterior of the models soft and made out of one material. After I was happy with the models and lighting in Blender, I popped the graphics into Photoshop to touch up on colors and contrast. These are the final results.

Blender > Photoshop

For the second project, I wanted to do something off the beaten path to show a bit of my personality. While I am not a skateboarder nor a rollerskater, I like the idea of classic street shoes on skates. â€”I have to admit this was inspired by one of my USC professors who reinvigorated Vans' creative direction in the early 2000's. Being in his class, I was inspired by his passion for intersections in culture and how that can be marketed to mass audiences.

While making these assets, I had a pretty clear idea of visual direction. At first, I wanted to make some more 3D models and compile them into a video. But I quickly realized how that would infringe upon time efficiency. So, I grabbed some assets off of the internet and arranged them in Photoshop so that they aligned with my vision in terms of camera angle and lighting.

One thing that I love about Vans is their ability to produce marketing materials that others would find risky. Even their logo has a hand-drawn appeal to it. I wanted to preserve that authentic human feel in the creation of these assets. In order to do that, I found a font that really felt like a human wrote it out. Then I added a few details with the brush tool, making sure that the layout of the text was in congruence with the background images and overall just felt right. After tweaking and editing for a little while, I got to my final products. They can be seen below.
